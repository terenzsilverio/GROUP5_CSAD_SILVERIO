The Selfish Gene

Richard Dawkins

“Unfortunately, however much we may deplore something, it does not stop being true.”

Originally published in 1976, Richard Dawkins’ book argues that genes are the basic unit of evolution, not individual organisms or even species. Due to their naturally selfish behavior, genes merely use organisms as mechanisms to ensure their own survival. Different body features, behaviors, and even altruistic actions are nothing but tools for them. And yet, sometimes animals and humans escape this disillusioning picture.
