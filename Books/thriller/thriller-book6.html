The Silence of the Lambs

Thomas Harris

“When the Fox hears the Rabbit scream he comes a-runnin', but not to help.”

The book opens with Agent Jack Crawford pulling Clarice Starling out of training for a special assignment. As his personnel are busy working on the investigation of Buffalo Bill, Crawford asks Starling to help complete the FBI’s profiling database by interviewing imprisoned serial killer Dr. Hannibal Lecter. At the Baltimore State Hospital for the Criminally Insane, Clarice meets the arrogant administrator Dr. Chilton and the cunning Lecter. Lecter refuses to complete her questionnaire, but when another inmate assaults Clarice, Lecter gives her a clue to follow. Tracking down the lead, Clarice finds the preserved head of “Klaus”—a lover of Lecter’s former patient and victim—in a storage unit. Clarice returns to Lecter for more information, but Lecter wants to discuss Buffalo Bill.
